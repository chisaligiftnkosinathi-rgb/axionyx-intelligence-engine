# Scientific Information Architecture (SIA)

Scientific Information Architecture is the discipline of designing scientific information systems whose structure preserves evidence, provenance, coherence, institutional memory, and human understanding through adaptive computational architectures.

AXIONYX applies principles analogous to Intelligent Architecture to scientific information systems. Rather than optimizing for appearance or centralized control, it emphasizes coherence, evidence preservation, human-centered workflows, modular adaptation, and institutional memory. This approach treats scientific information architecture as an adaptive, evidence-centered system whose integrity emerges from coherent relationships rather than predetermined forms.
